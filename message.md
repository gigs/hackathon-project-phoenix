Observation & setting the scene
We have lost some customer centricity. Not in behavior - but in systems and processes. We lack a single view of a customer and what are the current priorities for that customer. The moment a different customer-facing team becomes in the lead (IM / marketing / partner management), the source of information and structure of information changes.
We are not creating the right visibility for other parts of the organization on customers and their plans. Founders for one - but product and engineering have consistently given this feedback too. Arguably: we are not even creating the right visibility within Growth.
Linear is a dead end for info and is rarely used by founders to get status updates on projects - while it is positioned as source of truth to the teams. This creates a self fulfilling prophecy. Sharing in Slack is just way more impactful, so people will continue sharing in Slack before info reaches Linear, and Linear will feel like overhead most of the time (or is deprioritized). 
On top of this, there are ad-hoc meeting forums where we are repackaging info that lives elsewhere in Slack / Linear for a specific purpose (was definitely true for State, lesser extent functional sync). 
Different teams are trying to solve this, in parallel - e.g. automated account overviews from Louisa, or automated project updates in Linear from Max, etc. We don't need to push structure to the teams, we just need to get people in the room who know their stuff and give them the mission to converge on a customer level. 
We should embrace LLMs here. This is all about converting messy human work to structured info, which is exactly what LLMs are great at. 

Proposed approach:
Prioritize a centralized customer view, that works across functions. See attached last version of the mock-up. 
Have it sourced by Linear + Lightdash, maintained by humans that are heavily supported by AI.
Use the central customer view for all reporting on customers to drive behavior. 
More details last section.
